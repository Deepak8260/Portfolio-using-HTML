  2. Analysis and Visualization of Global Population Data in 2021

    1. Analyzed global population data by country for 2021 using Python to visualize population distribution, identify the top and least populous countries, and create various plots for better insights.

    2. Tools used: Python, Pandas, Plotly.

    3. Data preparation involved loading the dataset, filtering relevant columns ('Country Name' and '2021' population), renaming for clarity, and cleaning population values for analysis.

    4. Visualized data using:

      1. A histogram showing most countries with smaller populations and a right-skewed distribution.

      2. A bar chart of the top 10 most populous countries, led by China and India with over 1.3 billion people each.

      3. A list of the 10 least populous countries, with Vatican City having the smallest population of 803 people.

      4. A box plot highlighting the wide range of population sizes and outliers.

      5. A choropleth map displaying population density across regions, showing high concentrations in Asia and the Americas.

      6. A pie chart illustrating that the top 10 countries account for about 56% of the global population, with China and India making up nearly 40%.

    5. Demonstrated skills in data cleaning, visualization, and demographic analysis using Python libraries.
